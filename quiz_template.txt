NurseQuest Quiz Template
=======================
Use this template to create questions in a TXT or Word document.
Each question starts with a number (e.g. 1.) or a type tag (e.g. [Matching]).
Leave a blank line between questions. 

Multiple Choice (MCQ) — Default Format (No type tag required)
--------------------------------------------------------------
1. What is the normal resting heart rate range for an adult?
A) 40-60 bpm
B) 60-100 bpm
C) 100-120 bpm
D) 120-150 bpm
Answer: B
Explanation: The normal resting heart rate for adults ranges from 60 to 100 beats per minute.

True / False Question
----------------------
[TrueFalse]
2. Hand hygiene is the single most important practice in reducing infection transmission.
Answer: True
Explanation: Hand hygiene is the cornerstone of clinical infection prevention.

Matching Pairs Question
------------------------
[Matching]
3. Match the medical condition with its primary diagnostic indicator:
Diabetes = Polyuria
Hypertension = Elevated blood pressure
Hypothyroidism = Elevated TSH levels
Explanation: Polyuria is common in diabetes; elevated BP in hypertension; elevated TSH in hypothyroidism.

Sequence / Procedure Ordering
------------------------------
[Sequence]
4. Order the steps for clean dressing change:
1) Perform hand hygiene
2) Apply clean gloves
3) Remove old dressing
4) Clean wound site
Explanation: Hand hygiene must always be done first, followed by glove application, removal, and cleaning.

Jumbled Letters (Vocabulary Unscramble)
----------------------------------------
[Jumble]
5. Unscramble the term representing slow heart rate:
Word: BRADYCARDIA
Explanation: Bradycardia refers to a heart rate below 60 beats per minute.

Slider (Numeric Range Question)
--------------------------------
[Slider]
6. What temperature defines a fever in Celsius?
Min: 35  Max: 41  Step: 0.1  Answer: 38
Unit: °C
Explanation: A body temperature of 38°C (100.4°F) or above is clinically considered a fever.

B) Left Ventricle
C) Right Atrium

9. Identify the lung sound heard in the recording:
Media: wheezing.mp3
A) Clear vesicular
B) Coarse crackles
C) High-pitched wheezing

Explanation: High-pitched whistling sounds on expiration represent wheezing.
