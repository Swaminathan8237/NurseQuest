────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 1
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Kahoot! AS
Year              : 2013

Methodology       : Game-based learning platform using a host-device model.
                    Teachers launch quizzes on screen; students respond via
                    personal devices in real time using a join PIN.

Datasets          : 300 million+ registered users globally; used across
                    K-12, higher education, and corporate training environments.

Results/Outcome   : Demonstrated significant improvement in student engagement
                    and short-term knowledge recall in classroom settings.

Advantage         : Simple, intuitive UI; real-time leaderboard; broad device
                    compatibility; widely adopted in education worldwide.

Disadvantage/GAP  : No nursing-specific content or clinical question types;
                    no XP/rank progression system; no role-based educator
                    access; lacks media-rich clinical scenarios (ECG, audio).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 2
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Quizlet Inc.
Year              : 2005

Methodology       : Spaced-repetition and active recall via digital flashcards.
                    Students self-study using study modes (Learn, Match, Test).

Datasets          : 500 million+ study sets created by users; 60 million
                    active learners across 130+ countries.

Results/Outcome   : Improved memorisation of terminology and definitions;
                    effective for vocabulary-heavy subjects like anatomy.

Advantage         : Easy flashcard creation; collaborative study sets;
                    multiple study modes; supports images in cards.

Disadvantage/GAP  : No real-time multiplayer or live competitive sessions;
                    no gamification engine (XP, badges, levels); not designed
                    for clinical scenario-based assessment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 3
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Moodle Pty Ltd (Open Source)
Year              : 2002

Methodology       : Learning Management System (LMS) built on PHP. Delivers
                    course content, quizzes, forums, and grading through
                    a structured course-module architecture.

Datasets          : Used by 300 million+ users in 250+ countries; deployed
                    across universities, hospitals, and government institutions.

Results/Outcome   : Provides comprehensive course management; supports
                    blended and online learning with detailed progress reports.

Advantage         : Open-source and free; highly extensible via plugins;
                    supports SCORM, rich media, peer assessment, and grading.

Disadvantage/GAP  : Heavy and complex server setup; no built-in gamification
                    or competitive gameplay; poor UI/UX for student engagement;
                    lacks real-time quiz session capability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 4
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Google LLC
Year              : 2012

Methodology       : Web-based form/survey tool adapted for quiz delivery.
                    Auto-grading via multiple-choice and short-answer types.

Datasets          : Used by millions of educators globally; integrated with
                    Google Workspace (Classroom, Sheets, Drive).

Results/Outcome   : Simple, fast quiz creation with automatic grade export
                    to Google Sheets for teacher review.

Advantage         : Zero cost; no account needed for respondents; instant
                    result collation; integrates with Google Classroom.

Disadvantage/GAP  : No gamification; no live multiplayer; no media-rich
                    clinical question types; no student progression tracking,
                    streaks, or achievement system.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 5
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Mentimeter AB
Year              : 2014

Methodology       : Interactive slide-based presentation tool using live
                    audience polling, word clouds, and Q&A widgets embedded
                    into teacher-driven slideshow sessions.

Datasets          : Used by 300 million+ participants across 200+ countries
                    in corporate and academic presentation settings.

Results/Outcome   : Increases audience participation and live feedback
                    during lectures; reduces passive listening in classrooms.

Advantage         : Visually appealing real-time responses; easy to embed
                    in presentations; supports anonymous participation.

Disadvantage/GAP  : Not a dedicated quiz/assessment platform; no scoring
                    system, no XP or levels, no curriculum-linked assessments,
                    and no nursing domain-specific content.

ROW 6
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Wayground (EdTech Startup)
Year              : 2019

Methodology       : Online assessment and analytics platform with configurable
                    test forms, auto-grading, and student dashboard reporting.

Datasets          : Used in select universities and training centers; primarily
                    corporate and professional certification environments.

Results/Outcome   : Enables structured online assessments with dashboard
                    analytics for tracking student performance over time.

Advantage         : Customisable assessments; decent analytics; integration
                    support with popular LMS tools; automated grading.

Disadvantage/GAP  : No gamification or competitive elements; no live
                    multiplayer mode; generic content with no nursing or
                    clinical domain specificity.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 7
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Nearpod Inc.
Year              : 2012

Methodology       : Teacher-paced interactive lesson delivery platform.
                    Lessons include embedded quizzes, polls, VR experiences,
                    and collaborative boards synced to student devices.

Datasets          : Used in 15,000+ schools; 2 million+ teachers; primarily
                    K-12 in the US; integrated with Google Classroom & Canvas.

Results/Outcome   : Improved student attention and lesson comprehension;
                    real-time teacher visibility of student responses.

Advantage         : Rich lesson formats (VR, 3D, video); real-time student
                    response monitoring; integrates with major LMS platforms.

Disadvantage/GAP  : Primarily a lesson delivery tool, not a standalone
                    competitive quiz game; no XP system, no student rank
                    progression, no nursing-specific content.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ROW 8
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : Socrative Inc. (acquired by Mastery Connect)
Year              : 2011

Methodology       : Classroom response system using teacher-created quizzes,
                    exit tickets, and team "space races" for formative
                    assessment in real time.

Datasets          : Used by 2 million+ teachers in 150+ countries; primarily
                    higher education and secondary school environments.

Results/Outcome   : Effective formative assessment tool; instant class
                    performance reports; identifies student knowledge gaps.

Advantage         : Simple quiz creation; real-time feedback; team Space
                    Race adds competitive element; downloadable reports.

Disadvantage/GAP  : Limited question types (MCQ, T/F, short answer only);
                    no media-rich clinical scenarios; no gamification engine
                    or XP/badge progression system for nursing education.

ROW 9
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Author/Developer  : ProProfs (Merger of Snow Technologies)
Year              : 2006

Methodology       : Online quiz maker with branching logic, timed tests,
                    certificates of completion, and performance analytics
                    dashboard for instructors.

Datasets          : Used by 15 million+ users; deployed in corporate training,
                    higher education, and professional certification programs.

Results/Outcome   : Enables self-paced online assessment; auto-generates
                    certificates; provides detailed student performance reports.

Advantage         : Rich quiz creation options; branching logic; certificate
                    generation; white-label options; strong analytics.

Disadvantage/GAP  : No nursing-specific domain modules; no live multiplayer
                    mode; limited question types; subscription-based pricing
                    limits accessibility for academic institutions.
