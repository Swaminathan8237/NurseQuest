A 58-year-old male presents to the Emergency Department complaining of crushing sub-sternal chest pain radiating to his left shoulder, accompanied by diaphoresis and acute dyspnea. His initial ECG shows ST-segment elevation in leads V1 to V4.

According to emergency protocols, which of the following is the most immediate independent nursing intervention?
ECG MONITOR: ANTERIOR STEMI PATTERN
A
Initiate high-flow oxygen via non-rebreather mask at 15 L/min.
B
Establish IV access and administer Morphine Sulfate 2 mg IV push.
C
Administer Aspirin 325 mg PO to be chewed immediately.
D
Prepare the patient for urgent surgical coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG).
check

EXCELLENT CHOICE!

Aspirin 325 mg PO (chewed for rapid absorption) is the highest priority immediate independent intervention for acute STEMI to prevent further platelet aggregation and thrombus propagation.

close

INCORRECT RESPONSE

The correct answer is C. Aspirin PO (chewed) is the primary first-line independent action. Oxygen is only indicated if SaO2 < 90%, and Morphine requires a physician's order.
